LLM + Digital Twin Emergency Repair Decision System

Type Power Industry Competitive Project
Duration 2024-2026
Role Principal Investigator
¥1.806M Funding

Project Overview

This groundbreaking project integrates Large Language Models (LLM) with Digital Twin technology to create an intelligent emergency repair decision-making system for power grid infrastructure. The system provides real-time situational awareness and automated response recommendations during critical infrastructure failures.

Business Value

Emergency response automation and intelligent decision support dramatically reduce downtime, improve operational efficiency, and ensure faster recovery from infrastructure failures.

Key Technologies

Large Language Models (LLM)

Advanced natural language processing for understanding emergency reports, generating repair protocols, and providing human-like decision support.

Digital Twin Technology

Real-time virtual replica of power grid infrastructure enabling simulation, prediction, and scenario analysis for repair planning.

Knowledge Graph

Structured representation of equipment relationships, failure modes, and repair procedures for intelligent reasoning.

Multi-modal Data Fusion

Integration of sensor data, weather information, maintenance records, and field reports for comprehensive situational awareness.

System Capabilities

  • Intelligent Incident Analysis: Automatic parsing of emergency reports and extraction of critical information
  • Real-time Digital Twin Simulation: Live modeling of grid state and failure propagation
  • Automated Repair Recommendations: AI-generated repair strategies with resource requirements
  • Natural Language Interface: Conversational interaction for field engineers and operators
  • Predictive Impact Assessment: Forecast of repair outcomes and service restoration time
  • Historical Knowledge Integration: Learning from past incidents and best practices

Expected Impact

• Response Time Reduction: 60% faster emergency response through automated analysis

• Decision Accuracy: 90%+ accuracy in repair strategy recommendations

• Operational Efficiency: 35% improvement in resource allocation during emergencies

• Knowledge Retention:> Systematic capture and reuse of institutional repair expertise
